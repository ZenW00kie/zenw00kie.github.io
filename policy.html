Fun Fact

The term "bug" to describe a flaw in a computer system was popularised by U.S. Navy Rear Admiral Grace Hopper in 1947. After her team found a moth stuck in a relay, physically impeding the operation of the Harvard Mark II computer, they taped the insect to the logbook with the note: "First actual case of bug being found."
