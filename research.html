Research Portfolio

My research bridges the gap between the theoretical and the practical, combining rigorous academic inquiry with a deep understanding of real-world operational and policy constraints. I focus on developing security solutions that are not only technically sound but also usable, effective, and aligned with the strategic objectives of organisations in critical sectors.


In the high-stakes environment of healthcare, security measures must enhance, not hinder, clinical workflows. My research in this area focuses on understanding the security perceptions and practices of clinicians through multi-national studies. By identifying the friction points between existing security controls and the realities of patient care, I develop actionable recommendations for designing and implementing security solutions that are both effective and empathetic to the needs of healthcare professionals. This work also extends to patients, examining how they weigh abstract cybersecurity risks against concrete clinical benefits in the context of informed consent for connected medical devices.

Effective security must be baked in, not bolted on. My research focuses on the real-world application of secure-by-design principles, with a particular emphasis on threat modeling. Through in-depth qualitative studies with industry practitioners, my work deconstructs how security experts actually approach design analysis, revealing a more natural, ad-hoc workflow than what is prescribed by standard methodologies. These insights have led to the development of the Threat Modeling Naturally Tool (TMNT), a first-of-its-kind platform designed to support a more intuitive and effective approach to building secure systems from the ground up.

In today's complex software supply chain, timely and accurate vulnerability intelligence is critical. My research in this area focuses on the security advisory ecosystem, analyzing the availability, consistency, and utility of public vulnerability data. I have designed and built a large-scale OSINT platform to aggregate and standardize security advisories from hundreds of sources, creating a unique dataset for analyzing vulnerability trends and developing more effective severity metrics. This work has direct implications for both public policy and enterprise risk management, and I am an active collaborator with the CISA Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure team.

My background in government and national security provides a unique lens through which I view the challenges of cybersecurity. I believe that effective security is not just a technical problem, but a strategic one, deeply intertwined with policy, regulation, and organisational behaviour. My work seeks to inform the development of more effective security policies and strategies by grounding them in a deep understanding of the operational realities and human factors at play. Whether it's advising on regulatory frameworks for medical devices or developing strategies for securing critical infrastructure, my goal is to provide evidence-based guidance that is both pragmatic and impactful.
